The 7 CCSP Exam Traps That Catch Everyone — Including People Who Already Have Their CISSP

Published February 27, 2026 · 8 min read

You'd think having a CISSP would make the CCSP easy. Same organization (ISC2). Overlapping domains. Similar question style.

You'd be wrong.

I passed the CISSP in 2024 and walked into the CCSP expecting a cloud-flavored victory lap. Instead, I encountered seven specific traps that nearly derailed me.

Trap #1: Assuming Cloud Security = Traditional Security in the Cloud

The CCSP doesn't ask "how do you do security, but in cloud?" It asks about security concerns unique to cloud computing. Multi-tenancy isolation. Data residency and sovereignty. CSP contract negotiations. Shared responsibility matrices. Vendor lock-in risk assessment.

Trap #2: The Legal/Compliance Domain Is Heavier Than You Think

Domain 6 (Legal, Risk, and Compliance) is where people bleed points. GDPR implications, data breach notification laws, eDiscovery in cloud environments, audit rights clauses in CSP contracts.

Trap #3: The "Correct" Answer Is Often the Most Conservative

The CCSP almost always favors more oversight and governance, keeping more control with the customer, prioritizing data protection, and including contractual protections.

Trap #4: Cloud Reference Architectures Are Testable

Know: NIST SP 500-292, CSA Cloud Controls Matrix, ISO/IEC 27017 and 27018, SOC 2 Type II reports.

Trap #5: Business Continuity in Cloud ≠ Traditional BC/DR

CSP outage scenarios, data portability, cloud-specific RPO/RTO, the difference between provider-managed and customer-managed DR.

Trap #6: Virtualization and Container Security Details

Hypervisor types, VM escape attacks, container isolation, serverless security.

Trap #7: The Exam Clock Is Your Real Enemy

150 questions in 4 hours. 1.6 minutes per question. Long multi-paragraph scenarios.

  4. Do NOT rely solely on CISSP knowledge
