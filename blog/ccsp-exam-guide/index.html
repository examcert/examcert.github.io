Everything You've Heard About the CCSP Is Wrong — Here's What the Exam Is Really Like

Published February 22, 2026 · 8 min read

"The CCSP is just a cloud version of the CISSP." Wrong.

"If you passed the CISSP, the CCSP will be easy." Very wrong.

"The CCSP is easier because it's narrower." Hilariously wrong.

I've passed both, and the CCSP surprised me more than any cert I've ever taken. Here's what nobody tells you about this exam — and how to actually prepare for it.

Myth 1: "It's Just CISSP But For Cloud"

The CISSP is a mile wide and an inch deep. The CCSP is a half-mile wide and three inches deep on cloud-specific topics. The overlap between the two exams is only 25-30%.

The CCSP dives deep into cloud architecture models, cloud data lifecycle, virtualization security, and cloud-specific legal frameworks that the CISSP barely touches. Thinking of it as "CISSP-lite for cloud" will get you a failing score.

Myth 2: "CISSP Holders Breeze Through It"

Approximately 32% of candidates pass on their first attempt. CISSP holders consistently struggle on three domains:

  • Domain 1: Cloud Concepts, Architecture and Design — cloud reference architecture, shared responsibility models, and cloud deployment specifics go far beyond CISSP coverage.
  • Domain 2: Cloud Data Security — data lifecycle management, encryption in multi-tenant environments, and cloud-specific DLP strategies are entirely new territory.
  • Domain 3: Cloud Platform and Infrastructure Security — hypervisor security, virtual network segmentation, and container orchestration security aren't covered in CISSP.

Having a CISSP is helpful — but it's not a shortcut.

Myth 3: "The CCSP Is Easier Because It Has Fewer Domains"

Six domains vs. eight — but domain count does not equal difficulty.

  • Domain 2 (Cloud Data Security, 20%) is the largest and most technical domain. It covers data classification, encryption, tokenization, masking, data retention, and deletion across cloud environments.
  • Domain 6 (Legal, Risk and Compliance, 13%) is the most conceptually dense. International privacy regulations, cloud contracts, audit requirements, and jurisdictional issues make this domain uniquely challenging.

Fewer domains means each one carries more weight. You can't afford to be weak in any of them.

What Actually Works for CCSP Prep

  1. Read the (ISC)² CCSP CBK — especially the cloud data security and legal/compliance chapters. This is your primary source of truth.
  2. Spend 40% of your study time on Domains 2 and 6 — these two domains account for 33% of the exam and trip up the most candidates.
  3. Use scenario-based practice questions — the CCSP tests your ability to apply concepts, not just recall them. CCSP practice questions on ExamCert — $4.99 lifetime access with a pass-or-refund guarantee.
  4. Study for at least 8 weeks — even with cloud experience, you need time to internalize the (ISC)² perspective on cloud security.

Should You Get the CCSP?

If you work in cloud security: yes. The cloud security market is projected to hit $77 billion by 2027, and the CCSP is the gold standard credential for cloud security professionals.

If you're choosing between the CCSP and CISSP: get the CISSP first. It's more broadly recognized, and the foundational knowledge will make your CCSP preparation smoother. Then add the CCSP to specialize.
