CCSP vs CISSP: One Costs Less, Takes Less Time, and Might Actually Be More Relevant in 2026. Guess Which One.

Published February 26, 2026 · 8 min read

I hold both the CCSP and the CISSP. I got the CISSP first because everyone told me to. Looking back, I would have done it the other way around. And I think most people in 2026 should too.

Controversial? Maybe. But hear me out.

The Quick Comparison

CCSPCISSP
FocusCloud securityGeneral information security
Questions150125-175 (adaptive)
Time4 hours4 hours
Cost$599$749
Experience Required5 years (1 year cloud)5 years (2 domains)
Pass Rate (estimated)~32% first attempt~30% first attempt
Average Salary$145K-$170K$131K-$175K

The salary ranges overlap. The difficulty is comparable. But the CCSP is $150 cheaper, and the experience requirements are slightly more flexible — one year of your experience needs to be in cloud security specifically, but the rest can come from any security domain.

Why CCSP Is More Relevant Right Now

In 2026, the question isn't "do we use the cloud?" It's "how do we secure what's already there?"

Gartner predicts that by 2027, over 85% of organizations will be cloud-first. Most of them are already there. What they don't have is enough people who understand cloud-specific security challenges: shared responsibility models, identity federation, cloud data protection, virtual network segmentation, container security.

The CISSP gives you a mile-wide overview of information security. The CCSP gives you that plus deep cloud security knowledge. In a market where every company is deploying workloads on AWS, Azure, or GCP, which do you think hiring managers are prioritizing?

The Six CCSP Domains

  1. Cloud Concepts, Architecture, and Design (17%)
  2. Cloud Data Security (20%)
  3. Cloud Platform and Infrastructure Security (17%)
  4. Cloud Application Security (17%)
  5. Cloud Security Operations (16%)
  6. Legal, Risk, and Compliance (13%)

Domain 2 (Cloud Data Security) is the heaviest and, in my opinion, the hardest. It covers data classification, encryption in transit and at rest, key management, data masking, tokenization, and data retention policies. If you work with cloud data at all, this domain alone is worth the price of admission.

What Makes the CCSP Hard

The pass rate tells the story: roughly 32% on the first attempt. That's brutal.

Three things make it difficult:

1. The breadth. Six domains covering everything from cloud architecture to legal compliance. You can't just know the technical stuff — you need to understand contracts, regulations, audit processes, and compliance frameworks.

2. The ISC2 mindset. Like the CISSP, the CCSP wants you to think like a security manager, not a security engineer. The "best" answer is often the one that involves assessing risk, consulting stakeholders, or reviewing policies — not the one that implements a technical fix.

3. Cloud-specific nuance. Questions about shared responsibility models are deceptively tricky. Who's responsible for what depends on the service model (IaaS, PaaS, SaaS) and the specific scenario. You need to reason through each question carefully.

The Study Plan That Got Me Through

Weeks 1-3: Read the Official ISC2 CCSP CBK Reference (or Ben Malisow's CCSP Study Guide — it's more readable). Take notes on each domain.

Weeks 4-6: Practice questions. This is where I made the biggest gains. CCSP practice questions on ExamCert were my daily routine — 50 questions every morning before work. The explanations for wrong answers taught me more than any book.

Weeks 7-8: Focus on weak domains. For me it was Domain 6 (Legal, Risk, and Compliance). I hate legal content. But 13% of the exam is 13% of the exam.

Week 9-10: Full-length practice exams. Two full 150-question timed tests in the final two weeks. Adjust study based on results.

The Exam Day Experience

150 questions in 4 hours. Unlike the CISSP's adaptive format, the CCSP is linear — you can go back and change answers, which is a huge psychological advantage.

My strategy:

  • First pass: answer everything I'm confident about, flag the rest
  • Second pass: work through flagged questions carefully
  • Third pass: review any remaining flags and make final decisions
  • I finished with 30 minutes to spare

The questions are scenario-based and lengthy. Read every word. The difference between "cloud service provider" and "cloud customer" in a question can completely change the correct answer.

Why You Should Get CCSP Before CISSP

If you're planning to get both eventually, here's my argument for CCSP first:

1. CCSP qualifies as a CISSP experience waiver. It's staying on the waiver list even after ISC2's April 2026 cuts. Get CCSP, then use it to waive one year of CISSP experience.

2. Cloud security skills are in higher demand right now. Job postings requiring CCSP have grown 28% year-over-year. CISSP demand is stable but not growing as fast.

3. The material is more practical. CCSP content maps directly to what cloud security professionals do every day. CISSP content is more theoretical and managerial.

The Bottom Line

The CCSP is harder than most people expect, more relevant than most people realize, and more valuable than most people give it credit for. In a cloud-first world, cloud security expertise isn't optional — it's essential.
