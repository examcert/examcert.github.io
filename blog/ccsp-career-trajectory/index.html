How Getting the CCSP Changed My Career Trajectory More Than Any Cloud Cert Ever Did

Published February 17, 2026 · 7 min read

Three years ago I had five AWS certs and was applying to the same roles as everyone else. Hundreds of applicants per posting. Ghosted by recruiters. The usual cloud engineer experience.

Then I got my CCSP, and within three months I had a new job with a 40% raise. Coincidence? Maybe. But let me tell you why I don't think so.

What the CCSP Actually Is

The Certified Cloud Security Professional (CCSP) is ISC2's cloud-specific security certification. Think of it as CISSP's younger, more specialized sibling.

Six domains: Cloud Concepts & Architecture (17%), Cloud Data Security (20%), Cloud Platform & Infrastructure Security (17%), Cloud Application Security (17%), Cloud Security Operations (17%), Legal/Risk/Compliance (13%).

125 questions. 4 hours. Passing: 700/1000.

Why It Hit Different

Every company is moving to the cloud, and every company is terrified about security. That intersection — cloud + security — is where the demand is explosive and the supply is thin.

AWS certifications prove you know AWS. But who proves you can secure any cloud environment? That's the CCSP holder.

I went from being one of 500 cloud engineers to being one of 50 cloud security architects.

The Study Approach That Worked

10 weeks, about 10 hours per week.

  • Weeks 1-3: Read the official ISC2 CCSP study guide cover to cover.
  • Weeks 4-6: Deep dive into weak domains. Mine was Legal/Risk/Compliance.
  • Weeks 7-9: Practice exams. CCSP practice questions on ExamCert — 50 questions every morning.
  • Week 10: Light review. One final full-length practice exam (86%).
