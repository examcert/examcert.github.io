Azure AZ-305 vs AWS SAP-C02: I Took Both Architect Exams in 2026. One Was Worth Every Penny. The Other Made Me Question My Life Choices.

Published March 12, 2026 · 6 min read

I took the Azure Solutions Architect Expert (AZ-305) in January 2026 and the AWS Solutions Architect Professional (SAP-C02) in February. Both are considered the pinnacle architect certifications in their ecosystems. Both are notoriously difficult. Both cost roughly the same.

But they're wildly different exams testing wildly different skills. Here's my honest comparison.

The Basics

AZ-305 SAP-C02
Questions 40-60 65
Time 120 minutes 180 minutes
Passing Score 700/1000 750/1000
Cost $165 $300
Prerequisite AZ-104 recommended SAA-C03 recommended
Case Studies Yes (2-3) No (but long scenarios)
Difficulty 7/10 9/10

Right off the bat, the SAP-C02 is more expensive, longer, has a higher passing score, and is generally considered harder. Fun.

How the Exams Think Differently

AZ-305: "Here's a company with these requirements. Design a solution using Azure services."

The AZ-305 gives you case studies — detailed company profiles with requirements, constraints, and existing infrastructure. Then it asks specific design questions. Which database? Which compute option? How should networking be configured?

It's very structured. Microsoft tells you what to design, and you pick the right components. Think of it like an architecture Lego set where you choose the right pieces.

SAP-C02: "Here's a nightmare scenario. Fix it. Also, consider cost, performance, security, and operational excellence simultaneously."

AWS throws complex multi-account, multi-region scenarios at you and expects you to balance five different concerns at once. There's no case study format — just long question stems that read like incident reports.

It's more chaotic and more realistic. Real architecture decisions involve painful tradeoffs, and AWS tests exactly that.

Where I Struggled on Each

AZ-305 weak points:

  • Azure App Configuration vs Azure Key Vault (when to use which)
  • Azure Front Door vs Traffic Manager vs Application Gateway
  • Azure Cosmos DB consistency models (session, bounded staleness, eventual, strong)
  • The case study format is time-consuming — I almost ran out of time

SAP-C02 weak points:

  • Multi-account architectures with AWS Organizations and SCPs
  • AWS Transit Gateway configurations
  • DR architectures across regions (pilot light vs warm standby vs active-active)
  • Everything about costs (Reserved Instances, Savings Plans, Spot, and when each matters)

Study Time Comparison

AZ-305: 6 weeks, approximately 10 hours per week. I already had my AZ-104, which helped enormously. Without the AZ-104 foundation, add another 4-6 weeks.

SAP-C02: 10 weeks, approximately 12 hours per week. Even with my SAA-C03, this exam demanded significantly more preparation. The breadth of services tested is simply larger.

Practice Exam Importance: Critical for Both

Both exams have question formats that differ from typical cert exams. The AZ-305 case studies require you to reference a company profile while answering — you need practice managing that context. The SAP-C02 questions are so long that time management becomes a skill in itself.

Which One Gets You Hired Faster?

Based on my job search experience and conversations with recruiters:

In enterprise environments: AZ-305 wins. Microsoft dominates enterprise IT, and Azure architects are consistently in demand at Fortune 500 companies. The AZ-305 + AZ-104 combination signals enterprise readiness.

In startups and tech companies: SAP-C02 wins. AWS still leads in cloud market share among tech-forward companies. Having the SAP-C02 signals you can design complex, scalable architectures.

In consulting: Get both. Consulting firms want architects who can work across cloud providers. Having both certifications makes you the multi-cloud unicorn every firm wants.

Salary Comparison

  • Azure Solutions Architect Expert: $140,000-$175,000 average
  • AWS Solutions Architect Professional: $150,000-$185,000 average

The AWS cert commands slightly higher salaries on average, but the difference narrows in enterprise environments where Azure is dominant.

My Recommendation

If you're choosing one: Pick the cloud your target companies use. Check their job listings. If they mention Azure more than AWS, get the AZ-305. And vice versa.

If you want maximum career flexibility: Get the AZ-305 first. It's cheaper ($165 vs $300), faster to study for, and the case study format teaches you structured design thinking that transfers to any cloud. Then pursue the SAP-C02 for the AWS side.

If you're early in your career: Neither. Start with associate-level certs (AZ-104 or SAA-C03) and get 1-2 years of hands-on experience. These architect exams assume you've already been building things in the cloud.
