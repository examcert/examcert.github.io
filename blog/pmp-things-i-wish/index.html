7 Things I Wish Someone Told Me Before Taking the PMP

February 15, 2026 · 10 min read · PMP Certification

I passed the PMP last month. Here are seven things nobody warned me about.

1. The PMP Is Not a Project Management Exam

It tests whether you can manage projects the PMI way. In real life, you do whatever works. On the PMP, you do what the PMBOK Guide says. Example: when a stakeholder adds scope, the correct answer is always "submit a change request through integrated change control."

2. It's 50% Agile Now

The exam was restructured in 2021. You need to understand Scrum, Kanban, when to use predictive vs adaptive approaches, and servant leadership.

3. The 35 Contact Hours Requirement Is Real But Flexible

A $15 Udemy course counts if it's 35+ hours. Don't overthink this.

4. The Exam Format Is Exhausting

180 questions. 230 minutes. Nearly four hours. Use both optional breaks.

5. "What Would You Do FIRST?" Is a Trap

PMI hierarchy:

  1. Assess/analyze
  2. Consult the plan
  3. Take action
  4. Update lessons learned

6. Earned Value Management Is Easier Than It Looks

CPI = EV/AC, SPI = EV/PV, EAC = BAC/CPI. Above 1.0 = good. That handles 80% of EVM questions.

7. Practice Tests Are Non-Negotiable

I did 1,500 practice questions total. Used ExamCert's PMP practice questions — closest to real exam difficulty.

Budget 8-12 weeks. Start with ExamCert to see where you stand.
